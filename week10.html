Research etc

According to Sergey Volokhin and Eugene Agichtein in their paper 'Understanding Music Listening Intents During Daily Activities with Implications for Contextual Music Recommendation' 96% of internet users consume licensed music, primarily via smartphones. They go on to state that most consumers discover new music via recommendations from streaming services such as Spotify and YouTube. Whilst I'm not against streaming services (I've benefitted from their recommendations in the past) I feel that these services (especially via smartphones) have spoiled the pastime of listening to music, particularly the album. It seems the convenience of streaming comes at the expense of a quality audio and visual experience. Remember buying an album on CD or vinyl and getting home to listen to it whilst poring over the sleeve notes and accompanying artwork?
